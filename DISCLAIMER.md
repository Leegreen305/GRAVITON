**This software is a theoretical physics simulation tool, not a blueprint for engineering.**

### What GRAVITON Is

GRAVITON implements equations from peer-reviewed physics literature (General Relativity, quantum field theory, gravitoelectromagnetism) to simulate spacetime geometries and exotic phenomena that are mathematically permitted by the field equations. Every equation in this codebase has a published citation.

### Key Caveats

1. **Exotic Matter**: The Alcubierre warp drive requires matter with negative energy density. While the Casimir effect demonstrates that negative energy densities exist in quantum field theory, no known method can produce macroscopic quantities of exotic matter.

2. **Energy Requirements**: Even the most optimistic estimates for warp bubble energy requirements exceed the total mass-energy of Jupiter. GRAVITON honestly computes and reports these requirements.

3. **Quantum Inequality Constraints**: Ford-Roman quantum inequalities place strict limits on the magnitude and duration of negative energy densities. GRAVITON includes these constraints in its exotic matter module.

4. **The Horizon Problem**: Superluminal warp bubbles are causally disconnected from their walls, making it impossible to create, control, or destroy the bubble from within. GRAVITON reports this limitation.

5. **Numerical Accuracy**: While GRAVITON uses high-precision numerical methods (DOP853 integrator, 1e-10 relative tolerance), results should be verified against analytical solutions where available.
